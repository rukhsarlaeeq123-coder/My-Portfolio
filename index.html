M.Phil Thesis 2024 – 2025

Why Has Pakistan Fallen Behind in Human Development Index?

Supervised by Dr. Hafiz Pasha & Dr. Muhammad Afzal

Investigated why Pakistan lags on the HDI, focusing on how public education expenditure shapes educational outcomes — using annual time-series data (1999–2023) from PRSP Reports, the Pakistan Bureau of Statistics, and the World Development Indicators.

  • Performed HDI decomposition comparing Pakistan with Bangladesh to isolate the relative contributions of education, health and income
  • Found education to be the largest contributor to Pakistan's lower HDI relative to Bangladesh
  • Applied OLS regression after diagnostic testing to confirm model assumptions were satisfied
  • Showed real current education expenditure significantly raises primary enrolment, while both current and development expenditure raise secondary enrolment
